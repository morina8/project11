About

It’s a class project. It’s also supposed to make sense.

This site is technically fictional, but the structure isn’t. The point here wasn’t to make something flashy or overdesigned. It was to build something that works the way people expect it to work. You open it, you understand it, and you don’t have to think too hard about what to click next.

Mission

Keep it simple and keep it usable. That’s really it. The idea is to give people a place where they can find what they need, read, or sit for a bit without the page trying to show off or get in the way. If you can move through the site without noticing the design, it’s doing its job.

Services

  • Borrow books, magazines, and audiobooks
  • Use computers and free Wi-Fi
  • Reserve a quiet study space
  • Join small reading groups and events

Consistent Layout

Everything looks the same from page to page on purpose. If you have to stop and figure out how a site works more than once, something went wrong.

Organized Structure

The files are where they should be. If you ever had to fix something, you wouldn’t have to dig through five layers of nonsense to find it.

Readable Design

This isn’t trying to be clever. It’s trying to be clear. You can scan it, find what you need, and move on without fighting the layout.
